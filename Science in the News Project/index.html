Sources of Information

The news article and your summary are examples of sources of scientific information, which can be categorized into three main groups:

Primary
Original research, data, and findings, written by the scientists who conducted the study.
Secondary
Summaries or analyses of primary research for scientists, policymakers, funders, and sometimes public audiences.
Tertiary
Provides a broad overview, context, or quick facts about a topic to the public.

Important Publication Readiness Rule

Before a summary is shared with others, it should be checked carefully. A chatbot summary may sound clear and polished, but that does not always mean it is accurate or reliable.


To be rated Ready to Publish, the chatbot summary must have a score of 15.


Needs Minor Revisions Before Publishing means the chatbot has scored below 15, but earn at least 2 points in each of these areas:

  • Covers the main ideas of the article
  • Use of evidence
  • Source reliability

If any of these areas receive a 1 or 0, it should be rated Needs Major Revisions Before Publishing.
