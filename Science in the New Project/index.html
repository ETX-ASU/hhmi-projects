Request the AI Summary

Use AI to generate a summary of your original science news article. Instead of copying a prompt, you’ll write your own. Keep in mind that the quality of the AI’s response depends on how clearly you communicate your expectations in your prompt.

Here are the goals your prompt should achieve:

  • Include the link to your original science news article.
  • Focus on the right content and audience.
  • Produce a clear and concise summary, 350 words or fewer.
  • Support the summary with credible scientific resources and their associated links.

AI follows your instructions. Before you submit your prompt, check:

  • Is it clear and specific?
  • Did I communicate what matters most?
  • Will it produce the kind of summary I want?

  • Push the chatbot to explain how it chooses words and builds responses step-by-step.

Analyze the Chatbot Summary

GenAI output is not 100% accurate. As the editor, make sure it's credible! Use lateral reading to check the references in the chatbot-generated summary article to verify whether they exist.

For each link in the article:

Open the link

  • Copy the title and search for it using Google or Google Scholar.

Confirm the source

  • Does the link lead to a real, relevant page?
  • What information does it provide?

Evaluate credibility

  • Who is the author? Do they have expertise?
  • Are there any clear biases or conflicts of interest?
  • Do other credible sources say the same thing?

Check sourcing

  • Is the original primary source included?

You stayed in control!

Throughout this activity, you guided AI, evaluated its accuracy, and used it to strengthen your own thinking and writing. AI can be a helpful tool, but it works best when you stay in control. Because it builds sentences based on patterns and probabilities, it can sound confident while still missing details or making small mistakes. That’s why it’s important to read AI-generated summaries critically, checking for accuracy, clarity, and completeness.

The real strength of AI isn’t just how powerful it is, but how you use it. When used well, AI can help sharpen your ideas, not replace them.
